How to read crochet terms in patterns for beginners

If you are a beginner crochet artist you might be confused by some of the terms used in crochet tutorials. This blog is going to help you understand some of the most commonly used terms and what they mean.


  1. Chain (ch)


    The foundation of crochet that resembles a chain hence its name


  2. Yarn over(yo)


    This is wrapping yarn around your crochet hook


  3. Slip stitch (sl st)


    This stitch is commonly used to join rows. It is made by inserting your hook into a stitch then pulling through all the hoops on the hook.


  4. Single crochet (sc)


    A single crochet, abbreviated by sc, is a stitch made by inserting your hook into a stitch, yarning over, pulling up a loop, yarning over again and pulling through the remaining two loops.


  5. Half double crochet


    This stitch is slightly tall than the single crochet. To make this stitch, yarn over, insert your hook into a stitch, yarn over again then pull through all the three loops on the crochet hook.


  6. Double crochet


    A double crochet is like two single crochets rolled into one. To create one, yarn over, insert your hook into a stitch, yarn over, pull through one hoop, yarn over, pull through two, yarn over then pull through the remaining two. This stitch is taller than a single and half double crochet making it effective if you want to finish your project within a short time.


  7. Treble crochet(tr)


    Of the stitches I've mentioned so far, a treble crochet is the tallest of them all. Abbreviated by (tr) this stitch is made by yarning over two times, inserting your hook into a stitch, pulling through one, yarning over, pulling through two loops, yarning over, pulling through two loops, yarning over and pulling through two loops again.


  8. Magic ring


    A magic ring comes into play when someone wants to create something with a circular base. It is mostly used when making hats/beanies among others.


  9. Increase(inc)


    To make an increase means to increase the number of stitches in a row. To do this you add the number of stitches you wish to add, it can be one or two or any other number, into a single stitch.


  10. Decrease(dec)


    This is the opposite of an increase. I'm going to use a double crochet stitch to explain this one: yarn over, insert your hook into a stitch, yarn over, pull through one, yarn over, pull through two and instead of yarning over and pulling through the remaining two like in a normal dc, you are going to yarn over, insert your hook into the next stitch, pull through one, yarn over, pull through two, yarn over then pull through the remaining three.


  11. Stitch marker


    This is something used to mark where a specific action should be done so that the crochet artist does not forget. It does not necessarily have to be one bought from a crochet supplies store. It can even be your loop earing!


These are some of the most commonly used terms and abbreviations. There are many more and as you proceed you'll know more about them. Thank you for reading.
